Back Pain in Nigeria: Why It Is Epidemic and How to Fix It Without Surgery

Back pain is one of Nigeria's most common health complaints. Here is the truth about what causes it and what actually fixes it.

✍ NaijaBuzz Staff•June 2026•5 min read

Back pain has become one of the most common health complaints among working-age Nigerians, and the combination of increasingly sedentary urban lifestyles, long hours sitting in traffic, the prevalence of desk work, and the heavy physical labour that characterises many informal sector jobs has created conditions where spine health problems affect a significant and growing proportion of the population. The World Health Organisation identifies lower back pain as the leading cause of disability worldwide, and Nigeria's urban workforce is experiencing this burden acutely.

The overwhelming majority of back pain experienced by Nigerians — estimated at over 85% of all cases — is non-specific lower back pain, meaning it has no identifiable structural cause such as a disc herniation, fracture, or tumour. It is caused by a combination of muscle weakness, prolonged static postures, sudden movements, and the accumulated stress of repetitive positions on a spine whose supporting muscles have not been adequately conditioned. This type of back pain is both preventable and treatable through relatively simple interventions, yet most Nigerians who experience it either endure it passively, self-medicate with analgesics, or seek injections and surgery that are inappropriate for their condition.

The most evidence-based treatment for non-specific lower back pain is movement — specifically, graded physical activity that gradually strengthens the muscles supporting the spine while maintaining the range of motion that static rest progressively reduces. The instinct to rest completely when back pain strikes is understandable but counterproductive; prolonged bed rest for non-specific back pain is now known to worsen outcomes rather than improve them. Short periods of rest for acute severe pain are appropriate, but returning to gentle movement — walking, stretching, and progressively increasing activity — within 24 to 48 hours consistently produces better results than extended rest.

Core strengthening exercises — targeting the abdominal, gluteal, and deep spinal muscles that collectively support the lumbar spine — are the most effective long-term intervention for both treating and preventing back pain. Exercises including the plank, bridge, bird-dog, and dead bug can be performed anywhere without equipment and take less than 20 minutes daily. YouTube channels dedicated to back pain rehabilitation provide free, properly demonstrated routines. A consistent programme of core strengthening performed three to four times weekly produces measurable improvements in back pain and functional capacity within four to six weeks for most people.

Ergonomic improvements for desk workers address the postural causes of work-related back pain. Adjusting chair height so that feet rest flat on the floor and knees are at 90 degrees, positioning the computer screen at eye level, and using a lumbar support cushion to maintain the natural curve of the lower back reduce the postural stress that accumulates over long sitting hours. Standing up and moving for at least five minutes every hour — setting a phone alarm as a reminder if necessary — interrupts the static loading that is the primary mechanism of desk-related back pain. These adjustments cost nothing and produce immediate and lasting improvements for the majority of Nigerian desk workers experiencing back discomfort.

🔑 Key Takeaway: Most Nigerian back pain is caused by prolonged sitting, weak core muscles, and poor posture. Regular movement, core strengthening exercises, and ergonomic adjustments resolve 90% of cases without surgery or injections.
