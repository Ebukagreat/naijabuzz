How to Start an E-Commerce Business in Nigeria and Actually Make Sales

Millions of Nigerians are trying to sell online but most make very little. Here is what separates successful Nigerian e-commerce sellers from those who struggle.

✍ NaijaBuzz Staff•June 2026•7 min read

Nigerian e-commerce has experienced explosive growth over the past five years, driven by rising smartphone penetration, improving logistics infrastructure, and a generation of consumers increasingly comfortable making purchases online. The total value of Nigerian e-commerce transactions exceeded $12 billion in 2025, and the trajectory continues steeply upward. For entrepreneurs looking to participate in this growth, the opportunity is real — but so is the competition, and understanding what actually drives sales success in the Nigerian online market is essential before investing time and capital.

The platform decision — where to sell — is the first and most consequential choice for any new Nigerian e-commerce seller. Jumia and Konga offer marketplace access to large existing customer bases but charge commissions of 10 to 20% and subject sellers to platform policies that can be restrictive. Instagram and WhatsApp — the dominant social commerce channels in Nigeria — offer direct customer relationships and zero platform fees but require the seller to build their own audience and manage their own customer service entirely. A personal website on Shopify or WooCommerce provides the most control but requires the most marketing effort to generate traffic. Most successful Nigerian online sellers use a combination — social media for discovery and relationship building, WhatsApp for transaction completion, and marketplace presence for credibility.

Product selection is where most Nigerian e-commerce businesses are won or lost before a single sale is made. The products that sell best online in Nigeria share several characteristics: they are difficult or inconvenient to find in local physical markets, they have clear visual appeal that translates well to product photography, they are light enough that delivery costs do not make the price uncompetitive, and they solve a problem or satisfy a desire that a large segment of the population shares. Fashion accessories, beauty products, phone accessories, home organisational products, and children's educational materials are categories that consistently perform well in the Nigerian online market.

Product photography is the single most impactful investment a Nigerian online seller can make after selecting a good product. The gap between a product photographed on a plain background in good natural light and the same product photographed on a cluttered surface in poor lighting is the difference between a sale and a scroll-past. A ring light — available for ₦5,000 to ₦15,000 — and a plain white or grey backdrop transform product photography quality dramatically. Showing products in use — being worn, being applied, being operated — rather than only as static objects increases purchase intent significantly. And consistent visual branding across all product images builds the recognition that makes customers return.

Customer service is the most underrated competitive advantage in Nigerian e-commerce. The most common complaint about Nigerian online shopping is not price or selection but reliability — orders that do not arrive, sellers who go silent after payment, products that do not match their descriptions. The seller who responds to every inquiry within two hours, provides accurate delivery timelines and communicates proactively when delays occur, and handles complaints and returns fairly without argument will build a loyal customer base and powerful word-of-mouth referrals in a market where genuine reliability is genuinely rare. In Nigerian e-commerce, trust is the product. Everything else is just merchandise.

💡 Key Takeaway: Pick one product category you understand deeply, master one sales channel before expanding to others, and invest in product photography and customer service before spending on ads. Trust takes time to build online.
