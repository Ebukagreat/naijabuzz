Hidden Nigeria: 10 Stunning Places in Nigeria That Most Nigerians Have Never Visited

Nigeria is more beautiful than most Nigerians realise. Here are 10 destinations that will make you see your country differently.

✍ NaijaBuzz Reporters•May 2026•6 min read

Ask most Nigerians where they would travel if money were no object and the answers cluster around London, Dubai, Paris, and New York. Ask them to name five beautiful places within Nigeria and many struggle to get past Lagos, Abuja, and perhaps the Obudu Cattle Ranch. This is not ingratitude — it is a reflection of how poorly Nigeria's extraordinary natural and cultural heritage has been marketed and made accessible to its own citizens. The country is, in truth, staggeringly beautiful. Here are ten places that prove it.

1. Obudu Mountain Resort, Cross River State — Sitting 1,576 metres above sea level in the Obudu plateau, this destination offers temperatures that drop to 16 degrees Celsius, mist-covered valleys, waterfalls, and a cable car that was once rated among the most scenic in the world. It feels nothing like the Nigeria most visitors expect. 2. Yankari National Park, Bauchi State — Home to the largest population of elephants in West Africa, hippos, baboons, and over 350 bird species. The warm springs at Wikki — crystal clear water emerging from the earth at a constant 31 degrees — are among the most extraordinary natural phenomena in Nigeria.

3. Erin Ijesha Waterfall (Olumirin), Osun State — Seven tiers of waterfalls cascading through rainforest, accessible by a trek that rewards the effort with some of the most spectacular scenery in southwest Nigeria. 4. Idanre Hills, Ondo State — Ancient granite hills rising dramatically from the Ondo plain, with 676 steps leading to a plateau that contains historic settlements, a mysterious floating rock, and views that stretch to the horizon. 5. Cross River National Park — One of the last remaining areas of tropical rainforest in West Africa, home to critically endangered Cross River gorillas, chimpanzees, and forest elephants.

6. Ikogosi Warm Springs, Ekiti State — A natural phenomenon where warm and cold springs meet at a single point, surrounded by a resort and rainforest. 7. Zuma Rock, Niger State — A monolithic rock formation rising 725 metres, considered one of the largest monoliths in the world and visible from 50 kilometres away. 8. Lekki Conservation Centre, Lagos — Nigeria's longest canopy walkway, suspended 22 metres above a wildlife conservation area within Lagos itself. 9. Ngwo Pine Forest, Enugu State — A temperate pine forest that feels entirely unlike the rest of Nigeria, with waterfalls, caves, and cool air that provides a genuine escape from urban heat. 10. Badagry, Lagos State — A haunting and historically significant town that was one of the primary departure points for enslaved Africans during the transatlantic slave trade, with museums, monuments, and beaches that carry the weight of history alongside their natural beauty. Nigeria contains multitudes. Most of them are waiting to be discovered.

✅ What This Means For You: Nigeria has world-class natural beauty waiting to be explored. Plan a domestic trip this year — you may be surprised by what your country has to offer.
