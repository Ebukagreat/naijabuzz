Traditional African Foods That Scientists Now Say Fight Cancer

New research confirms what our grandmothers knew — moringa, egusi, and bitter leaf pack serious health benefits.

✍ NaijaBuzz Staff•May 2026•6 min read

For generations, Nigerian grandmothers have sworn by the healing power of bitter leaf soup, moringa tea, and egusi. They were dismissed as old wives' tales by a world rushing toward pharmaceutical solutions. But now, a growing body of scientific research is validating what African traditional knowledge has long maintained — many of our native foods contain powerful compounds that actively fight cancer, inflammation, and disease.

Moringa oleifera, known in Nigeria as ewe igbale, has been the subject of over 1,300 scientific studies. Researchers at the University of Lagos and international institutions have found that moringa leaves contain isothiocyanates — compounds that have demonstrated the ability to inhibit the growth of cancer cells, particularly in breast, liver, and colorectal cancers. A single cup of moringa tea delivers more vitamin C than seven oranges and more calcium than four glasses of milk.

Bitter leaf, or Vernonia amygdalina, is another powerhouse that science is now taking seriously. Studies published in the African Journal of Traditional Medicine found that bitter leaf extracts significantly reduced tumor size in laboratory models of breast cancer. The plant contains vernodalin and vernomygdin — bitter compounds that trigger apoptosis, the natural process by which the body destroys abnormal cells before they become dangerous.

Egusi seeds, the melon seeds used widely in Nigerian soups, are rich in conjugated linoleic acid and tocopherols — forms of vitamin E that have been linked to reduced risk of prostate and breast cancer. Meanwhile, tiger nuts, or ofio in Yoruba, contain resistant starch that feeds beneficial gut bacteria, reducing inflammation throughout the body — inflammation being a key driver of most chronic diseases including cancer.

The message from science is now clear: the African diet, when prepared traditionally and consumed regularly, is one of the most powerful preventive medicine systems in the world. Before Nigeria fully abandons its food heritage in favour of processed imports, perhaps it is time to look again at what has been on our plates for centuries — and recognize it for the medicine it truly is.

💡 Key Takeaway: Moringa, bitter leaf, egusi, and tiger nuts contain proven cancer-fighting compounds. Make them a regular part of your diet.
