Travelling From Nigeria on a Budget: How to See the World Without Breaking the Bank

Travelling internationally from Nigeria seems expensive but it is more affordable than most people believe. Here is how to do it smartly.

✍ NaijaBuzz Staff•May 2026•6 min read

The perception that international travel is beyond the reach of ordinary Nigerians is partly a function of real costs and partly a function of not knowing how to minimise those costs strategically. The naira-to-dollar exchange rate makes dollar-denominated travel expenses painful, and the Nigerian passport's visa requirements add complexity and cost that citizens of more powerful passports simply do not face. But none of these barriers makes international travel impossible for a middle-class Nigerian with reasonable planning, savings discipline, and the right information.

Flights are typically the largest expense in international travel from Nigeria, and booking strategy makes an enormous difference to the price paid. Research consistently shows that booking 8 to 12 weeks in advance produces significantly lower fares than last-minute purchases. Travelling midweek — particularly Tuesday and Wednesday departures — is typically cheaper than weekend travel. Setting fare alerts on Google Flights, Skyscanner, or Kayak for your intended route allows you to book when prices drop rather than at the moment you decide to travel. And being flexible about departure airports — Lagos versus Abuja versus Port Harcourt — can produce meaningful savings on specific routes.

Accommodation costs can be dramatically reduced by choosing self-catering apartments through Airbnb or Booking.com over hotels, particularly for stays of more than three or four nights. Hostels — which have high-quality options in most major travel destinations including London, Dubai, and Accra — offer dramatically lower per-night costs than hotels while providing opportunities to connect with other travellers. And travelling in groups — sharing an Airbnb apartment between four to six people — reduces per-person accommodation costs to levels comparable with mid-range hotels in Nigeria.

The visa process is the most administratively complex aspect of international travel for Nigerian passport holders, but it is manageable with preparation. Most Nigerian travellers begin with visa-free or visa-on-arrival destinations — Kenya, Ghana, Rwanda, Benin Republic, Seychelles, and several others — before tackling the more complex applications for UK, Schengen zone, or US visas. When applying for these more demanding visas, the most important factors are demonstrating strong ties to Nigeria — employment, property, family — that motivate return, and providing clear, consistent documentation of finances and purpose of travel. Applications prepared with genuine supporting documentation are approved far more frequently than those rushed or incomplete.

The financial preparation for international travel should begin at least six months before the intended travel date. Dedicated savings into a locked account, converted to dollars progressively as the exchange rate allows, builds travel funds gradually without requiring a single large conversion at an unfavourable rate. Travel insurance — frequently overlooked by Nigerian travellers — is non-negotiable for any serious trip and costs far less than the expenses it covers if medical emergencies or flight cancellations occur. The world is more accessible from Nigeria than the barriers suggest. The Nigerians who travel widely are not necessarily richer — they are better prepared.

💡 Key Takeaway: Book flights 8 to 12 weeks in advance, travel during off-peak seasons, use Booking.com for accommodation, and always apply for your visa well ahead of time. Budget travel from Nigeria is absolutely achievable.
