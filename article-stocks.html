How to Start Investing in Nigerian Stocks With as Little as ₦5,000

The Nigerian stock market is accessible to anyone with a smartphone and ₦5,000. Here is how to get started.

✍ NaijaBuzz Staff•May 2026•6 min read

Many Nigerians believe that investing in the stock market is only for the wealthy — something reserved for big businessmen and retired civil servants with millions sitting idle. This belief is not just wrong; it is costing ordinary Nigerians the opportunity to grow their money while they sleep. The Nigerian Exchange Group is accessible to anyone with a smartphone, a bank account, and as little as ₦5,000 to start.

The first step is opening a Central Securities Clearing System account, which is the digital system that holds your shares. This is done through a stockbroker. In 2026, several app-based brokers make this process entirely digital and free. Platforms like Bamboo, Trove, and Chaka allow you to open an account in minutes, fund it with your debit card, and start buying shares of Nigerian companies immediately.

For beginners, the safest approach is to start with well-established blue-chip stocks. Companies like Dangote Cement, MTN Nigeria, Zenith Bank, and Airtel Africa have consistent track records of paying dividends and growing in value over time. These are businesses that Nigerians interact with every day, making them easier to understand and monitor. A simple strategy of buying ₦5,000 worth of these stocks every month — regardless of what the market is doing — is called dollar-cost averaging, and it has created wealth for millions of investors worldwide.

The NGX All Share Index has delivered an average annual return of approximately 22% over the past five years, significantly outperforming savings accounts and even many fixed deposit products. While past performance does not guarantee future results, the long-term trajectory of Nigerian equities has rewarded patient investors handsomely.

The biggest mistake new investors make is waiting until they have enough money to start. The truth is that the best time to start investing was yesterday, and the second best time is today. Download Bamboo or Trove, open your account this weekend, and put in whatever you can afford. Your future self will thank you.

💡 Key Takeaway: Use Bamboo, Trove, or Chaka to start investing in Nigerian stocks today. Start with ₦5,000 and buy consistently every month.
