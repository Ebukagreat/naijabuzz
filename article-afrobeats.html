Afrobeats Goes Global: How Nigerian Artists Are Conquering Europe and Asia

Burna Boy, Asake, and a new wave of Nigerian artists are selling out arenas on every continent.

✍ NaijaBuzz Staff•May 2026•5 min read

Ten years ago, if you played an Afrobeats song at a club in Tokyo or Berlin, you would be met with puzzled looks. Today, those same clubs have dedicated Afrobeats nights that sell out weeks in advance. The global takeover of Nigerian music is not a trend — it is a cultural revolution, and it is only accelerating.

Burna Boy's 2025 world tour grossed over $120 million, making him one of the highest-earning touring musicians on the planet regardless of genre. Asake sold out the O2 Arena in London three nights in a row — a feat that took artists like Ed Sheeran years to achieve. Davido's collaboration with American and European artists has introduced Afrobeats rhythms to audiences who had never considered African music before. And Tems, with her soulful blend of Afrobeats and R&B, has become a genuine global superstar with fans from Seoul to Sao Paulo.

The numbers tell the full story. Afrobeats streaming on Spotify grew by 550% between 2020 and 2025, making it the fastest-growing music genre globally. In South Korea — one of the world's most music-obsessed nations — Nigerian artists now regularly appear on mainstream radio. In France, Afrobeats has overtaken American hip-hop as the dominant genre in urban clubs. The world is not just tolerating Nigerian music; it is addicted to it.

Behind the music is a sophisticated industry that Nigerian entrepreneurs have built largely from scratch. Record labels like Mavin, Spaceship, and YBNL have developed world-class production capabilities in Lagos. Music video directors like TG Omori have created a visual language for Afrobeats that is as compelling as the sound. And a new generation of Nigerian music managers and lawyers are negotiating deals with Sony, Universal, and Warner that were unimaginable a decade ago.

Nigeria has always known its music was special. Now the world knows too. The next generation — artists currently recording in bedrooms in Surulere, Oshodi, and Port Harcourt — are already preparing the next wave. Afrobeats did not peak. It is just getting started.

💡 Key Takeaway: Afrobeats is now the world's fastest-growing music genre. Nigerian artists are building a billion-dollar cultural export.
