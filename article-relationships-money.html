Money and Relationships in Nigeria: How to Have the Conversations That Matter

Financial disagreements are the leading cause of relationship breakdown in Nigeria. Here is how to have the money conversations that actually matter.

✍ NaijaBuzz Staff•May 2026•6 min read

Research consistently identifies money as the leading cause of conflict in Nigerian marriages and relationships — ahead of infidelity, communication problems, and family interference. Yet despite its centrality to relationship health, financial conversations remain among the most avoided topics in Nigerian courtship and family life. Young couples who discuss every aspect of their relationship in exhaustive detail before marriage frequently never have a single honest conversation about debt, savings, income, spending habits, or financial goals. The consequences are predictable and painful.

The cultural factors driving this avoidance are real. In many Nigerian traditions, discussing money openly — particularly income — is considered bad luck, a sign of distrust, or simply impolite. Women who ask questions about a partner's finances risk being labelled as gold-diggers. Men who admit to financial struggles risk being perceived as inadequate providers. These social scripts make honest financial communication genuinely difficult, even between people who love each other and are planning a life together.

But the cost of avoidance is high. A partner who discovers after marriage that their spouse has significant debt, a different approach to saving, or incompatible financial priorities faces a choice between conflict and suppression — neither of which leads to a healthy relationship. Extended family financial obligations — the expectation that successful family members will financially support relatives — are a source of enormous tension in Nigerian marriages when not discussed and agreed upon before commitment.

The conversations worth having before committing to a serious relationship include: What is each person's current financial situation — income, debts, savings? What are the financial obligations to extended family, and how will these be managed after marriage? Will finances be joint, separate, or a combination? What are each person's financial goals — property, children's education, retirement? And what happens when financial hardship hits, as it inevitably does?

These conversations are not romantic. They are necessary. A relationship built on financial honesty and aligned values is far more resilient than one built on romantic feeling alone. The Nigerian cultural wisdom that a good partner is not just someone you love but someone you can build with contains a financial truth that too many couples discover only after the building has already fallen down.

✅ Worth Remembering: Talk about money with your partner before marriage, set clear expectations with family, and treat financial conversations as acts of respect and care rather than conflict.
