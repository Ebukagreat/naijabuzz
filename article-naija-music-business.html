How to Make Money in the Nigerian Music Industry Without Being a Musician

Nigeria's booming music industry creates enormous wealth for people who never sing a single note. Here are the opportunities most people overlook.

✍ NaijaBuzz Staff•May 2026•6 min read

When Nigerians think about the music industry, they think about artists — the Burna Boys and Asakes who fill arenas and collect Grammy nominations. But for every successful artist, there is an ecosystem of professionals making substantial income from the music business without ever stepping on a stage. In 2026, as the Nigerian music industry generates billions of naira annually, the opportunities for non-performers have never been greater.

Music production is the most technically demanding and financially rewarding behind-the-scenes role. A top-tier Nigerian music producer charges between ₦500,000 and ₦5 million per beat, and the most in-demand producers have waiting lists that stretch months. The path to becoming a producer is more accessible than ever — digital audio workstations like FL Studio and Ableton Live have democratised music production, and countless tutorials are available for free. The investment is time and creative dedication rather than expensive studio equipment.

Artist management is another high-earning role that requires business acumen, relationship skills, and an eye for talent rather than musical ability. A manager typically takes 15 to 20% of an artist's earnings across all revenue streams — shows, endorsements, streaming, and merchandise. For a mid-level Afrobeats artist earning ₦50 million annually, that represents ₦7.5 to ₦10 million for the manager. The role involves booking shows, negotiating deals, building media relationships, and guiding the strategic development of the artist's career.

Music video direction has become one of the most lucrative creative careers in Nigeria. Directors like TG Omori have built both cultural influence and significant wealth through their visual work. A mid-range music video in Nigeria costs between ₦3 million and ₦20 million to produce, of which the director typically receives 20 to 30%. Learning cinematography, video editing, and visual storytelling — skills increasingly accessible through online courses and affordable equipment — opens a pathway into this high-demand field.

Event promotion, music blogging, social media marketing for artists, and music licensing are additional avenues through which Nigerians are building careers and incomes in the music ecosystem. The Nigerian music industry is a genuine economic sector, not just a cultural one. And like any growing sector, it rewards people with skills, relationships, and the initiative to create value within it — regardless of whether they can hold a tune.

📍 Key Takeaway: Music production, artist management, event promotion, and music video direction are lucrative careers in Nigeria's booming music industry that do not require you to be a performer.
