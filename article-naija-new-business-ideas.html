20 Profitable Business Ideas for Nigeria in 2026 That You Can Start This Month

Looking for a business to start in Nigeria right now? Here are 20 profitable ideas you can launch this month with minimal capital.

✍ NaijaBuzz Staff•June 2026•7 min read

The most common obstacle between Nigerians and entrepreneurship is not capital, education, or connections — it is the paralysis of not knowing where to start. With too many potential business ideas and too little clarity about which ones are genuinely viable in the current Nigerian environment, many potential entrepreneurs remain employees longer than they need to. This list cuts through the noise with twenty business ideas that are proven to generate income in Nigeria in 2026, require modest startup capital, and address real needs in the market.

1. Tutoring and lesson preparation services — WAEC and JAMB tutoring demand is constant and well-paying. 2. Phone screen repair — Every neighbourhood has thousands of cracked screens needing fixing; equipment costs under ₦100,000. 3. POS agent services — The Central Bank of Nigeria actively promotes financial inclusion through POS agents who earn per transaction. 4. Laundry and dry cleaning pick-up — Premium laundry delivery services charging ₦3,000 to ₦8,000 per load are in high demand in urban areas. 5. Virtual assistant services — Remote administrative support for Nigerian and foreign businesses via WhatsApp and email requires no capital beyond a smartphone.

6. Event photography and videography — A good smartphone camera and basic editing skills are sufficient to start. 7. Cake and pastry business — Nigerian celebration culture creates constant demand for custom cakes. 8. Interior decoration consultancy — Growing middle class investing in home aesthetics; consulting requires only design knowledge and a portfolio. 9. Hair braiding and styling — Consistent demand with relatively low startup costs for equipment. 10. Car wash and detailing — High vehicle ownership in urban areas creates daily demand; a mobile setup reduces overhead costs significantly.

11. Grocery delivery service — Convenience is increasingly valued by urban Nigerian professionals. A basic delivery operation requires only reliable transport and supplier relationships. 12. Social media management for small businesses — Most Nigerian small businesses need social media help but cannot afford agencies. 13. Website building for local businesses — Basic WordPress sites for restaurants, salons, and shops; template-based sites can be built in days. 14. Print-on-demand clothing — Custom printed t-shirts, hoodies, and accessories through local print shops or international services. 15. Mini importation and resale — Sourcing products from Alibaba and reselling through Instagram and WhatsApp.

16. Daycare and after-school care — Working Nigerian parents consistently need reliable childcare; home-based operations have low startup costs. 17. Generator repair and maintenance — Every Nigerian household with a generator needs regular servicing; a skilled technician can build a loyal client base quickly. 18. Recycling collection — Companies like Wecyclers pay for sorted recyclables; building a collection route in your neighbourhood generates income while addressing environmental problems. 19. Online resale of secondhand electronics — Buying and refurbishing used phones, laptops, and electronics for resale has strong margins for those who develop product assessment skills. 20. Meal prep and healthy food delivery — Growing health consciousness among urban Nigerians creates demand for nutritious prepared meals delivered to offices and homes.

The common thread connecting all twenty of these businesses is that they solve real problems that real Nigerians are already paying to have solved. None requires rare skills or connections. All of them can be started with less than ₦200,000 — and most with significantly less. The businesses that succeed from this list will not be those started by the most talented entrepreneurs but those pursued most consistently by people who showed up every day and delivered what they promised. Start small, execute well, and let the results speak for themselves.

🔑 Worth Remembering: The best business to start is the one you will actually execute. Pick one idea from this list that matches your skills and available capital, commit to it for six months, and build from there.
