How to Navigate Nigeria's Healthcare System and Get the Best Treatment Available

Finding good healthcare in Nigeria requires strategy, knowledge, and the right connections. Here is exactly how to do it.

✍ NaijaBuzz Reporters•May 2026•6 min read

Navigating Nigeria's healthcare system successfully requires knowledge, preparation, and a realistic understanding of what the public and private sectors can and cannot deliver. The public sector — federal teaching hospitals, state general hospitals, and primary healthcare centres — offers theoretically low-cost care that is in practice often limited by chronic underfunding, staff shortages, and equipment failures. The private sector offers better infrastructure and more reliable service but at costs that are significant for most Nigerian households without insurance.

The most important healthcare preparation any Nigerian can make is registering with a Health Maintenance Organisation before falling sick. HMOs like Reliance HMO, Hygeia, and Avon Healthcare offer plans that provide access to networks of private hospitals and clinics at predictable annual costs. Individual plans start from as low as ₦15,000 annually, family plans from ₦50,000. The coverage is not comprehensive — most plans have limits on specialist care, surgery, and hospitalisation — but having any HMO cover dramatically reduces the out-of-pocket cost of routine medical care and minor emergencies.

Identifying the best healthcare facilities in your area before you need them is another critical preparation step. Quality varies enormously between private hospitals even within the same city. Asking colleagues, neighbours, and social media communities for recommendations, visiting a facility before an emergency to assess cleanliness, equipment, and staff responsiveness, and identifying which hospitals have 24-hour emergency services and blood banks in your vicinity are activities that take little time but can make an enormous difference when urgency strikes.

For specialist care — cardiology, oncology, neurology, orthopaedics — Nigeria's federal teaching hospitals, despite their challenges, often have the most experienced specialists in the country. The trick is navigating the system effectively. Having a general practitioner who can write a referral letter, arriving early for appointments, building relationships with medical staff through respect and genuine engagement, and following up persistently rather than passively waiting are behaviours that get better outcomes from the public specialist system.

Medical tourism — travelling to India, Ghana, or South Africa for procedures unavailable or unreliable in Nigeria — is a reality for many Nigerians who can afford it. Indian hospitals in particular offer world-class treatment for complex conditions at costs significantly lower than Western healthcare while maintaining high standards. For cardiac surgery, cancer treatment, and complex orthopaedic procedures, medical tourism is worth serious consideration for those with the financial means. The key is research — identifying accredited hospitals, understanding total costs including travel and accommodation, and having a clear follow-up plan for post-treatment care back in Nigeria.

🔑 What This Means For You: Register with a reputable HMO, identify your nearest quality private hospital before an emergency occurs, and invest in regular preventive checkups. Preparation makes all the difference in Nigerian healthcare.
